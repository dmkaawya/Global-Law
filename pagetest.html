Key Fundamental Rights

From the Constitution of Sri Lanka

Art. 14

Equality

All persons are equal before the law and entitled to equal protection
Art. 12

Freedom from Discrimination

No citizen shall be discriminated based on race, religion, language, or sex
Art. 14(1)(a)

Freedom of Speech

Every citizen has the right to freedom of speech and expression
Art. 13

Freedom from Arbitrary Arrest

No person shall be arrested except according to procedure established by law
Art. 14(1)(g)

Freedom of Movement

Every citizen has the freedom of movement and residence within Sri Lanka
Art. 10

Freedom of Thought

Freedom of thought, conscience, and religion for every person

Fact Check

Common Legal Myths Debunked

❌

"Police can arrest anyone without a reason"

✅ False. Under Article 13, police must have reasonable suspicion or a warrant. Arbitrary arrest is unconstitutional and you can file a fundamental rights petition.

❌

"Online comments can't get you arrested"

✅ False. Under the Cyber Crime Act No. 24 of 2007 and the proposed Online Safety Bill, online harassment, hate speech, and defamation can lead to arrest and prosecution.

❌

"A verbal agreement isn't legally binding"

✅ Partially false. Verbal agreements can be legally binding, but harder to prove in court. For property and contracts over a certain value, written agreements are legally required.
