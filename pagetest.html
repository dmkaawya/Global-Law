🏛️

Supreme Court

ශ්‍රේෂ්ඨාධිකරණය • Article 105

Established
1972
No. of Judges
11 (incl. CJ)
Location
Hulftsdorp, Colombo

The Supreme Court is the highest and final court in Sri Lanka. It sits at the top of the judicial hierarchy and its decisions are binding on all other courts.

Established under the 1972 Constitution, its powers are defined in Article 105 of the 1978 Constitution. It has both original jurisdiction and appellate jurisdiction.

The Chief Justice and puisne judges are appointed by the President. The Supreme Court also has power to review legislation and determine whether laws are consistent with the Constitution.

📜

Constitutional Jurisdiction

Hears fundamental rights violations (Article 126), determines constitutionality of Bills, decides on constitutional interpretation referred by the President.

⚖️

Appellate Jurisdiction

Hears appeals from Court of Appeal in civil and criminal matters. Can grant leave to appeal. Issues Certiorari, Prohibition, Mandamus.

👑

Advisory Jurisdiction

President may refer questions of law of public importance for advisory opinion. Not binding but carries significant weight.

🔗

Revisionary Jurisdiction

Can call for and examine records from any court or tribunal. Can enhance or reduce sentences, order new trial.

🌍

Presidential Election Petitions

Exclusive jurisdiction to hear and determine election petitions regarding the election of the President.

1

File Petition

FR petition within 30 days of violation. For appeals, need leave to appeal first.

2

Notice to Respondents

Court issues notice to respondents. They file objections within granted time.

3

Hearing

Bench of 3-5 judges hears arguments. Attorney General may be heard in constitutional cases.

4

Judgment

Court delivers judgment. Can grant compensation, declarations, directives. Final and binding.

Court of Appeal

අභියාදන උසාවිය • Article 136

Established
1978
No. of Judges
11-12
President
President of CoA

The Court of Appeal is the intermediate appellate court. It hears appeals from High Court, District Courts, and certain Tribunals.

It does not hear cases for the first time — it reviews decisions to determine if there were errors of law or procedure.

Sits in benches of 3 judges. Can confirm, reverse, modify lower court decisions, or order a new trial.

🔨

High Court Appeals

Criminal appeals generally by right, civil appeals may require leave.

📋

District Court Appeals

Civil appeals, usually requires leave unless on a question of law.

⭐

Tribunal Appeals

Appeals from Labor Tribunals, Industrial Courts, and other tribunals.

📝

Writ Jurisdiction

Certiorari, Prohibition, Mandamus, Quo Warranto, Habeas Corpus.

1

Notice of Appeal

File within 14-30 days. Pay court fees and security deposit.

2

Record & Documents

Obtain certified copies. File appeal documents with grounds.

3

Hearing

Bench of 3 judges. Written or oral arguments.

4

Decision

Affirm, reverse, modify, or remit. Further appeal to SC requires leave.

High Court

මහා අධිකරණය • Article 138

No. of Courts
14
Zones
9
Judges per Court
1-3
Trial Type
Judge alone

The High Court is a court of first instance for serious criminal offences and also exercises appellate jurisdiction over Magistrate Courts.

Established under the Judicature Act No. 2 of 1978. Each High Court covers one or more districts. Tries murder, rape, large-scale fraud, dangerous drugs offences.

Jury trials were suspended in 1978. Currently most trials are by judge alone. Maximum penalties include death sentence and life imprisonment.

🔪

Original Criminal Jurisdiction

Tries offences punishable with death, life imprisonment, or imprisonment over 7 years under Penal Code, Firearms Ordinance, Dangerous Drugs Act.

📊

Appellate Criminal Jurisdiction

Hears appeals from Magistrate Court convictions. Can enhance, reduce, or alter sentences.

🔓

Bail Applications

Entertains bail in non-bailable offences where Magistrate refused. Broader bail powers.

📋

Limited Civil Jurisdiction

Election petitions, certain commercial disputes, specific matters under special statutes.

🔪

Murder & Culpable Homicide

Penal Code Sections 293-304. Death penalty or life imprisonment.

💊

Dangerous Drugs Offences

Possession, trafficking under Dangerous Drugs Act. Mandatory death for large quantities.

🔫

Firearms Offences

Unlawful possession, use, trafficking under Firearms Ordinance.

💸

Large-Scale Fraud

Cheating, misappropriation, forgery involving large amounts.

🚫

Rape & Sexual Offences

Grave sexual abuse, child abuse under Penal Code amendments.

🔥

Arson & Property Crimes

Arson, mischief causing damage exceeding threshold amounts.

🤝

Corruption & Bribery

Certain bribery cases under Bribery Act based on severity.

💻

Serious Cyber Crime

Serious computer offences under Computer Crimes Act referred by Magistrate.

📋

District Court

දිස්ත්‍රික් අධිකරණය • One per district

No. of Courts
54+
Court Type
Civil Only
Monetary Limit
No limit
Appeal To
Court of Appeal

The District Court is the primary civil court. At least one per district, with Colombo having several.

Hears all civil matters regardless of amount — land disputes, contract breaches, debt recovery, divorce, child custody, inheritance.

Cases can be filed where the defendant resides, where the cause of action arose, or where the property is located.

🏠

Land & Property Disputes

Ownership, partition, ejectment, boundary disputes.

💰

Debt Recovery

Loans, unpaid invoices, dishonoured cheques.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family & Divorce

Divorce (general law), alimony, child custody.

🔗

Inheritance & Succession

Letters of administration, probate, succession certificates.

📄

Contract Disputes

Breach of contract, specific performance, injunctions.

⚖️

Tort Claims

Negligence, defamation, nuisance, trespass.

🏢

Landlord-Tenant

Eviction, rent recovery under Rent Act.

Magistrate Court

මහේස්ත්‍රාත් අධිකරණය • Entry-level criminal court

No. of Courts
75+
Court Type
Criminal Only
Max Sentence
7 years / Rs. 15,000
Appeal To
High Court

The Magistrate Court is the lowest-level criminal court. Handles the vast majority of criminal cases.

Over 75 Magistrate Courts across the country. Magistrates are appointed by the Judicial Service Commission.

Maximum sentence: 7 years imprisonment and/or Rs. 15,000 fine. Serious offences are committed to High Court.

🍶

Minor Offences

Public nuisance, drunkenness, loitering, bylaw offences.

🤜

Simple Hurt & Assault

Simple hurt, criminal intimidation, wrongful restraint.

👛

Minor Theft

Theft below threshold, shoplifting, pickpocketing.

🚗

Traffic Offences

DUI, reckless driving, no license — Motor Traffic Act.

💻

Cyber Crimes (Minor)

Minor computer offences. Serious ones go to High Court.

📋

Summary Trials

Offences triable summarily — simplified procedure.

✅

Bailable Offences

Bail is a RIGHT. Magistrate MUST grant. Examples: minor assault, public nuisance, simple theft.

⚠️

Non-Bailable Offences

Bail at Magistrate's DISCRETION. Can refuse if risk of absconding or evidence tampering.

🚫

Unbailable Offences

Magistrate CANNOT grant bail. Must apply to High Court. Examples: murder, rape, certain drug offences.

🔄

Bail Refused? Your Options

1. Apply to High Court 2. File FR case if refusal unreasonable 3. Apply again with changed circumstances
